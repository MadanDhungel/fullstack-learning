Chicken Soup

Chicken soup is a warm, comforting dish enjoyed all over the world. It’s made by simmering chicken with vegetables, herbs, and spices, creating a flavorful broth that is both nourishing and soothing. Often considered a go-to remedy for colds and fatigue, chicken soup combines tender chicken pieces with ingredients like onions, carrots, garlic, celery, and seasonings.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (or 500–700 g chicken pieces with bone)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2–3 carrots, sliced
  • 2–3 celery stalks, sliced
  • 3–4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1–2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper (whole or ground)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh parsley or coriander (for garnish)
  • 6–8 cups water or chicken stock

Instructions

  1. Wash the chicken pieces well. If using a whole chicken, cut it into parts.
  2. In a large pot, add the chicken and pour in 6–8 cups of water (or chicken stock).
  3. Bring it to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Skim off any foam that rises to the top.
  4. Add the chopped onion, sliced carrots, celery, garlic, bay leaves, salt, and pepper.
  5. Let it simmer gently for 30–40 minutes (until the chicken is cooked and tender).
  6. Remove the chicken pieces from the pot, let them cool slightly, then shred the meat.
  7. Discard bones and skin, then return the shredded meat to the soup.
  8. If you want noodles, rice, or potatoes, add them at this stage and cook until soft.
  9. Adjust salt and pepper as needed.
  10. Add fresh parsley or coriander before serving.
  11. A squeeze of lemon juice can give extra freshness.
